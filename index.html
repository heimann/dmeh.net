Overview

I'm a computer programmer currently living in Brooklyn, NY.

Welcome to my corner of the internet. I won't make any promises about what you'll find here, but I generally try to make it a place that I would enjoy if I stumbled onto it myself. I also find it helpful to use it to clarify my own thinking, a sort of map for where I am and where I might be going.

Projects

brus.ai (current)

A platform for businesses to leverage LLMs to analyze their customer data.

links.rip (dormant-ish)

Where do all your links go? To die. Links.rip is my answer to bookmarking, a home for all the content that you stumble across on the internet, in a way that acknowledges that you'll probably never look at most of it. Think of it as bookmarking built around fuzzy search, if "bookmarking" was like sending a text message to yourself.

purple (acq by theSkimm 2018)

Purple originally built with a friend to cover the 2016 election over SMS let creators text with their audience and charge for it. It was a fun project, and I learned a lot from it, but it never really took off. We ended up selling it to theSkimm in 2018. I'm still proud of it, and I think it was a good idea, but it was a bit ahead of its time.
